Same Evidence, Different Answer: Auditing Order Sensitivity in Multimodal Large Language Models

Researchers have found that multimodal large language models (MLLMs) are not order-invariant, meaning that the order in which information is presented can affect the models' responses. This matters because standard benchmarks for MLLMs do not account for this order, which can lead to inconsistent results. The researchers developed a tool called Facet-Probe to audit the order sensitivity of 18 MLLMs, and found that response change rates range from 24% to 50%. This discovery is relevant to the development of more reliable and transparent language models, as it highlights the need to consider the impact of ordering on model performance.
