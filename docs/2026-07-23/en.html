LKValues: Aligning Large Language Models with Sri Lankan Societal Values

Researchers have introduced LKValues, a new approach to aligning large language models with Sri Lankan societal values, a crucial step in overcoming current cultural limitations, as existing language models tend to be biased towards Western norms. This is particularly relevant in multilingual societies such as Sri Lanka, where local values are unique and may be mishandled. LKValues was developed from a survey of 205 respondents, combining adapted global frameworks and local constructs elicited from language models, resulting in 40 majority-endorsed societal values. These values are used to create a Sinhala-English news-derived instruction corpus containing 150,000 scenario-based instructions.

Persian Pixel: A large-scale synthetic OCR dataset for Persian language

Researchers have created Persian Pixel, a large-scale synthetic dataset for Optical Character Recognition (OCR) in Persian, aiming to overcome the limitation of high-quality data for this language, spoken by over 110 million people across multiple countries. The intrinsic complexity of the Perso-Arabic writing system and the lack of large-scale, annotated datasets have been significant challenges for developing Persian OCR technologies. The Persian Pixel dataset can help fill this gap, providing a valuable tool to improve the accuracy of text recognition in Persian. This is particularly important, as Persian script exhibits features such as obligatory cursive connectivity, context-dependent glyph shaping, and stylistic variations.

PG-KINN: A Physics-Informed Petrov-Galerkin Kolmogorov-Arnold Network for Solving Forward and Inverse PDEs

A new approach to solving partial differential equations (PDEs) has been proposed, using a neural network called PG-KINN, which combines physics-informed learning with the Petrov-Galerkin form, allowing for a more accurate and interpretable solution of forward and inverse problems. This matters because current approaches, such as multilayer perceptrons (MLPs), have limitations due to their spectral bias and dense parameterization. The PG-KINN network uses learnable spline activations, which are structurally aligned with the piecewise-polynomial bases of classical discretizations, improving accuracy and interpretability. This new approach can be useful for solving complex problems in physics and engineering.

Statevector-Referenced Geometry Survival of a Four-Qubit ZZ Quantum Kernel on IBM Quantum Hardware: A Fixed-Subset Diagnostic Across Three Execution Configurations

A recent study published on arXiv investigated the survival of a four-qubit ZZ quantum kernel's geometry on IBM quantum hardware, which matters because quantum-kernel methods rely on the preservation of the dataset's geometry. The researchers measured the geometry's survival across three different execution configurations, using a frozen feature map and 24 indoor air-quality windows, and found that all configurations preserved the centered statevector geometry to a substantial, though incomplete, degree.
