Copy Less, Ground More: Overcoming Repetitive Copying in Long-Context Reasoning via Evidence-Aware Reinforcement Learning

Researchers have identified a critical issue in large language models that generate step-by-step reasoning traces: repetitive copying, where models extensively copy text from the input instead of productively solving the problem. This occurs because models fail to focus on relevant evidence for the task, copying indiscriminately from the prompt. To overcome this, the authors propose an evidence-aware reinforcement learning approach, which helps models focus on important information. This approach can improve model performance on complex tasks with long contexts, by enabling them to ground their reasoning more effectively.

OmniReasoner: Thinking with Long Audio-Video via Native Tool Use

Researchers have developed OmniReasoner, a post-training tool to help multimodal language models reason with long audio-visual content, overcoming current limitations due to the sparsity and cross-modal nature of crucial evidence. OmniReasoner works by building a low-cost global preview of the full stream and, when needed, calling a zoom-in tool for more detailed inspection of specific temporal intervals. This allows models to observe different sampling granularities before and after the call, improving decision-making capabilities. This approach may revolutionize how language models process multimodal information, by enabling more efficient and effective use of available data.
