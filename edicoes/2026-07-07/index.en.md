### [Deform360: A Massive Multi-view Visuotactile Dataset for Deformable World Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05390v1)
`arXiv`

A team of researchers has created Deform360, a large-scale visuotactile dataset for deformable object modeling, which can be crucial for robotic manipulation, as predicting object dynamics is a fundamental challenge, particularly for deformable objects, due to their high-dimensional state space and complex material properties. The Deform360 dataset contains 198 everyday objects, 1,980 interaction sequences, and over 215 hours of observations from 41 surround-view cameras and bimanual tactile grippers. This can help better understand the strengths and limitations of current world models, which approach deformable object dynamics in different ways, either by learning dynamics in 2D pixel space or in 3D geometric space, and can provide a systematic understanding of their relative strengths and limitations.

### [InFlux++: Real and Synthetic Data for Estimating Dynamic Camera Intrinsics](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05389v1)
`arXiv`

Researchers have released InFlux++, a new tool that combines real and synthetic data to estimate dynamic camera intrinsics, a crucial component for recovering 3D structure from 2D video. This matters because most 3D algorithms assume that camera intrinsics remain fixed throughout a video, an assumption that often fails for real-world videos. The InFlux++ aims to overcome the limitations of current methods, which are inaccurate due to scarce training data and a lack of scene and camera motion diversity. With InFlux++, researchers hope to improve the robustness of 3D methods in videos with dynamic intrinsics.
