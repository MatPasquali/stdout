### [Validity of LLMs as data annotators: AMALIA on authority](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08731v1)
`arXiv`

A recent study published on the arXiv repository investigated the validity of large language models (LLMs) as data annotators, using AMALIA, a 9 billion parameter national model for European Portuguese. What matters here is that AMALIA agrees with trained human coders on tasks involving the coding of the moral foundation of authority, achieving results close to those of larger models. However, agreement is not the same as validity, especially for theoretical constructs that need to be inferred, rather than read directly from surface features. The researchers tested the model's validity using the recovery gap, which measures the loss in performance when a holistic prompt is broken down into atomic clauses and recombined.

### [LongE2V: Long-Horizon Event-based Video Reconstruction, Prediction, and Frame Interpolation with Video Diffusion Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08770v1)
`arXiv`

Researchers have proposed LongE2V, a novel approach that leverages pre-trained video diffusion models to reconstruct, predict, and interpolate video frames from sparse event streams, a challenging task due to texture loss and long-term stability issues. LongE2V achieves high data efficiency and superior perceptual quality by fine-tuning a foundational video model. This is made possible through techniques such as Autoregressive Unrolling and Adaptive Context Switching, which mitigate temporal drift in extremely long sequences. The approach also introduces Reencoding Alignment with Cross Residual Correction to ensure precise bidirectional consistency during frame interpolation.
