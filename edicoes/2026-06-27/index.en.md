### [Hallucination in World Models is Predictable and Preventable](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27326v1)
`arXiv`

Researchers have found that hallucination in world models is predictable and preventable, which matters because these models are increasingly being used to generate action-controllable futures, but often produce unrealistic results. Hallucination occurs when models produce visually fluent rollouts that drift from the real dynamics. The researchers identified three distinct hallucination modes, namely perceptual, action-marginalized, and scene-diverging, and developed signals that can predict where the model will produce unrealistic results. This was made possible with the creation of MMBench2, a 427-hour, 210-task dataset for visual world modeling with ground-truth actions, rewards, and live simulators.
